Economy by a citizen

Posted on December 5, 2008 by tombuckelew

I am not an economist.  However, I am college educated, and have earned a Ph. D., and enjoyed more than 40 years of college teaching in the biological sciences.  I realize that there has been predatory lending amongst our financial institutions and that those recipients of the loans are somewhat at fault. Their fault is that they did not understand the stipulations that the banks had established.  Basically, they did not understand the fine print.  Who does?

The lending institutions realizing their own fault have sold that “bad paper” to higher-up institutions, literally passing bad loans on to others.  Those receiver institutions in turn have only sold the loans to institutions still higher in the food chain. 

All of us have been subject to chain mail, whether it is intended to gain the mailer money, prayers or freedom from guilt (for not breaking the chain).  This is no different.  The only people who gain (monetarily…can’t provide details on the prayer issue…nor can anyone else) are almost singularly at the first level of the chain.  By the time, the chain has run its course; the number of losers is infinitesimal. 

The big losers in this financial catastrophe are the huge companies who are ultimately left “holding the bag”.  However, the U. S. government has seen it desirable to bail out those companies despite the fact they are willing participants in a monumental  scheme (the term originates from its first perpetrator but most people recognize it as a Pyramid Scheme).  Any individual participating in such a scheme is usually prosecutable according to various state laws such as Missouri’s 407.400-407.420.

Our government has not only NOT prosecuted this practice but now is bailing out the losers at the bottom of the pyramid.  However, someone must lose in this pyramid scheme…and the loser…the US populace.  Well, not exactly.  I should have said the tax-payer populace, since the fat-cats have already gotten their monetary portion of the Ponzi/pyramid scheme before the dupes at the bottom of the pyramid have figured out that they have been had.

Those hapless individuals who began the process by innocently applying to become part of the American dream by owning their own homes are the real losers.  They are the low-to middle income wage earners whose only real guilt is that of being under-educated in financial matters; guilty of being gullible when they were told they were eligible for loans they didn’t think they could afford.  Many paid into the system until the interest rates rose to a point that made them realize the inevitable…that they had been had. 

Those individuals are the losers.  They paid into a system that was inevitably going to eat them alive.  Now eaten, they have become the losers.  However, the top of the pyramid (predatory lenders) has made its money and those recipients of that “bad paper” passed along to the bottom feeders of the Ponzi scheme have now been forgiven by the government. Who is going to rescue all those home owners whose mortgages have fallen into default?  Who is going to re-pay the lost equity as a consequence those predatory mortgages?

The government has in effect “socialized” the lending institutions.  We have heard time and time again about the evils of Socialism.  Yet, we have socialized local governments, socialized the airline industry, now socialized the banking industry.  Why not extend socialism to medicine?  Why not even…socialize the oil industry? Imagine not having to give the oil industry CEOs hundreds of millions of dollars per year…as bonuses. Imagine affordable gasoline at the pump.

Individuals have lost their homes, cars, credit and even jobs.  The predatory lenders have lost nothing.  The stooges who bought their predatory loans should have lost big…but our government has bailed them out.  The big losers are the homeowners who have defaulted on their mortgages but it is nothing compared to what America has lost. 

I drive through my home town and see “for sale” signs on virtually every block.  I know of people who are making mortgage payments on houses that will never be worth what they are paying for them.  I see homes boarded up that could be made livable.

Now, we are seeing our “big three” automakers on the verge of bankruptcy.  Whether the government bails them out or allows them to file for bankruptcy remains to be seen.  However, neither is the solution to the problem.  American automobiles have been eaten for lunch by the Asian makers.  I was always a Chevrolet or Ford owner until I bought my first Honda.  It was the best car I had until I more recently bought a Toyota Prius.  My wife and I love the Prius for its comfort as well as its economy.  Until, American automakers re-capture the confidence of Americans, they will continue to struggle.  The Chevrolet Volt, a plug-in due out in late 2009 is a good start.

The individual and the country were in excellent economic health in 2000.  We enjoyed a budgetary surplus for the first time in my lifetime.  America stood alone as the sole economic and military superpower in the world.  (Apparently we still maintain our role as the economic superpower since the actions of the past couple of months has had serious ramifications on the other world economies…e.g. Russia’s stock market has lost 50% of its value since May 2008 and during the late September crisis actually shut down for two days). China is beginning to see a turndown and subsequent civil unrest.  Our role as military superpower has seriously been affected by the misguided Iraq War.

The HIV/AIDS pandemic would greatly benefit from the development of a vaccine.  However, until the early 2000s, the US government had still not spent a billion dollars toward vaccine research in all the years of research to that point.  Pennsylvania’s roads are seriously in decline, requiring 1.7 billion dollars a year to maintain the bridges, secondary roads and turnpike.  The Iraq War is costing us 10 billion dollars a month (not to mention the cost of private contractors and stipends paid to the Sunni Awakening Group, formerly known as the Sunni insurgents.  The Sunni Awakening is the group which has made The Surge a success…we paid them off.  However, more recently, violence was surged once again because the majority Shiites have cut pay to the Sunni Awakening.)  My math tells me that paying for about five fewer days of the Iraq War could fund the highway infrastructure in Pennsylvania for a whole year. The 700 billion payout that our government has authorized in this latest buyout makes my HIV/PA roads argument petty.

Where does the money come from?  The answer is that you as a taxpayer will be ultimately responsible.  Well, not just you…since many of you will not outlive the debt that has befallen our country.  Your children, grandchildren and great-grandchildren will ultimately bear the burden of this responsibility.

No one thought Rome would ever fall.  It began with the excesses which included the Games of which the Coliseum was only the major leagues. Paying for all those gladiators (many were professionals), exotic animals and demonstrative displays helped to bankrupt the Roman Empire.  The size of the empire became too unwieldy to defend, taxing the army beyond its capability. The third nail in the coffin was a series of incompetent emperors.  It didn’t happen overnight.  It took a couple of centuries before bordering tribes saw a weakness and delivered the coup’de gras.

We have already experienced the “Games” portion, the overextension of our military and poor leadership.  However, I cannot see the Canadians or Mexicans taking over America.  But, these times are not as in Roman times.  A country can be overcome from afar by the simply key stroke on a computer.  Everyone has a credit card.  Everyone gets a bill which largely suggests satisfying the minimum payment…usually 15 dollars.  The majority of Americans are engaged in deficit spending and have a net savings of about minus1%.  Our government does exactly the same thing.  If China sells us a trillion dollars of goods, we hope to sell them a trillion in return.  However, the Chinese Yuan is devalued and therefore, the trade is uneven favoring China and increasing our deficit.  So, despite our government’s best intention of paying its monthly bill in full, it pays the minimum…the equivalent of your credit card companies’ 15 dollars.  The interest piles up and because we can’t pay, China buys in to our national debt.  It is difficult to get a handle on just how much of our national debt is owned by China but estimates are in the 20% level…with Japan holding a slight bit more.  What happens if Taiwan (our ally just off the cost of China) creates a problem and calls upon us for support?  Would China tell us to “pay up or shut up”?

President Bush first suggested that Social Security be nationalized in 2004.  Americans were smart enough to sound off and it was quickly moved to a back burner.    If individuals were left to deal with their own social security, we would have to re-invent the county homes for the indigent.  The events of the past months should illustrate that point. 

The solution to the social security problem is simple.  Most citizens erroneously believe that the money pulled from their weekly paychecks is invested in an account that they will be able to withdraw from when they reach their respective “social security “age. That isn’t the case.  The money coming out of your check is simply going to pay the current SS recipients.  So, as the population ages, there is a greater number of older people to pay for and fewer working people to provide the money.  I saw my obligation to social security rise as my salary rose.   

 The answer is to raise the ceiling on social security taxes and build up the surplus.  The ceiling is currently affixed at earnings of $102,000.  Allow those making between a $102,000 and $250,000 to enjoy a respite and then begin taxing at the $250,000 level to $300,000 level.  That would affect less than 1% of the US population.  Also make the government pay back the amount that it has borrowed from the fund, partly to finance the Iraq War. 

More importantly, the government continually tells us that SS will be broke by 2040.  A brief history lesson will tell us that in its incipience, SS had 15 people contributing for a single recipient.  Currently, 1.5 people now pay for one recipient.  It is a simple matter of demographics.  We have aged as a population but, as a population we will not age forever.  By 2040, all people deemed elderly now (including myself) will be dead, and the demographic shift will begin to reverse itself back toward the time of FDR.  The problem of social security should be solved and without all the hand wringing.

The remainder of the intelligence equation is affected by the nurturing that the individual has experienced. This suggests that providing a caring environment can more than make up for any genetic intellectual deficiencies. The statistics are not great but good enough to still indicate that environment plays a significant role in the development of intelligence and likely drive/incentive as well.
