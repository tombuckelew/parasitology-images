This is an enlarged view of Clonorchis sinensis at the midbody showing the tri-lobed ovary with the seminal receptacle (elongate with clear area around it).  The large, branched structure to the left is one of the testes and the two pairs of narrow lines at the top and bottom of the field are the caeca. In the upper portion of the photo, a fine line can be seen crossing the caecum at an oblique angle.  That is the vitelline duct, leading from that side of the body.   To the far right, the brown structure is the uterus, filled with recently-formed egg capsules beginning the journey to the uterine pore near the anterior end.  400x
